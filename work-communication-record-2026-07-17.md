# Lightpath Coaching Website - Work Communication Record

Date: 17 July 2026

This document summarises the website requirements, content decisions, and design direction discussed for the Lightpath Coaching website. It is written as a working communication record rather than a technical change log.

## Overall Website Direction

The website should remain simple, clear, and suitable for an early-stage tutoring business. The first version does not need complex functionality. The main user journey should be focused on families learning about the service and submitting a contact or booking enquiry form.

The design should feel warm, trustworthy, and appropriate for a small New Zealand-based education provider. The visual style should be brighter than the original version, but not overly white or washed out. The homepage should still have enough contrast and depth to feel professional.

## Home Page Requirements

The homepage should communicate the core value of Lightpath Coaching in a more direct and family-focused way.

The previous "What families notice" section was removed to make the page feel less generic and less repetitive.

The main support message should focus on practical outcomes for families:

```text
More support, greater confidence, real progress.
```

The supporting copy should explain that tutoring is built around the real academic and learning needs families care about:

```text
Our tutoring is designed around academic and learning needs that matter to families.
```

The testimonials section should be renamed from "What Parents Say" to:

```text
What Families Say
```

The supporting text should introduce the testimonials as real feedback from both parents and students:

```text
Real feedback from parents and students who have learned with us.
```

The testimonial content should be based on real parent and student feedback, but shortened and polished so it matches the tone of the website. Teacher names should not be emphasised in the testimonials, so the focus stays on the learning experience rather than one individual staff member.

The second testimonial should not overstate the University of Auckland admission result. It should instead focus on improved confidence, English ability, and feeling more prepared for university study.

## Homepage Visual Direction

The homepage hero image should better represent one-on-one or small-group tutoring. The preferred direction is a bright, natural, Kiwi-style learning environment with a tutor working closely with one or two students.

The selected image direction should feel:

- Natural and warm
- Suitable for New Zealand families
- Focused on small-group or personalised learning
- Professional but not overly corporate
- Bright, but not too white or faded

The final hero treatment should keep the darker original-style overlay, while using the newer small-group tutoring image. This keeps the page readable and professional while improving the relevance of the image.

## Tutoring Page Requirements

The tutoring page should be more specific and less generic. It should clearly show that Lightpath Coaching provides school-aged tutoring in Hamilton.

The page title should be:

```text
Hamilton tutoring for school-aged learners.
```

The phrase "and NCEA" should be removed from the title to keep it cleaner.

The local lesson location should be updated to:

```text
Chartwell Westfield
```

The "How it works" section should be clearer and more family-friendly:

- Step 1 should be titled "Book a Free Chat".
- Step 1 should use "the student's" instead of "your child's".
- Step 2 should be titled "Free Learning Assessment".
- The wording "confidence gaps" should be removed.
- Step 3 should use "Semi-private lessons" instead of "VIP paired lessons".
- Step 4 should be titled "Start Learning".
- Step 4 should explain that lesson schedules are recommended based on the student's goals and progress, and that many students attend two lessons each week.

The "Subjects" section should be renamed:

```text
Focused support for school learners.
```

References to "Years 9-13" should be removed from that section to avoid making the subject offering feel too narrow.

The "Lesson format" section should replace "VIP paired lessons" with:

```text
Semi-private lessons
```

The wording should explain this as a two- or three-student option for learners at a similar level who still need close tutor attention.

The "Standard learning schedule" block should be removed to keep the page simpler and avoid repeating information.

The final call-to-action section should remove the small green "Ready when you are" label. The main heading should become:

```text
Let's help every student feel confident.
```

## Booking Form Requirements

The booking/contact form should stay simple and easy for families to complete.

The "Parent name" field should not be required, so the asterisk should be removed.

"Child year level" should be renamed to:

```text
Student year level
```

The year level dropdown should include an "Other" option.

Lesson option wording should also match the updated site language, including "Chartwell Westfield" and "Semi-private lesson".

## Meet Our Team Page Requirements

The team page should be renamed or presented as "Meet Our Team" rather than focusing only on tutors, because the page includes both teaching staff and a marketing officer.

The page should not be split into separate tutor and staff sections. Instead, each person should be introduced with their role clearly shown on their card.

The team card layout should stay concise. Cards should show the person's name, role, focus area, photo, and a "Learn More" control. Detailed information should appear only after expanding the card, so the page feels cleaner and less crowded.

When one card is expanded on desktop, the other cards should not stretch awkwardly or create empty blank space. The expanded content should behave independently and keep the grid visually balanced.
